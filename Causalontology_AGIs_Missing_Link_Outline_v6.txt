THE MANIFESTO - OUTLINE (v6)

For THE AGI 2026 conference, San Francisco.

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator and Owner of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Working thesis, held as the frame of every chapter: Causalontology is important to achieve Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) - not as one more model, but as the missing DATA STRUCTURE: causation itself, reified into signed, mergeable, inspectable objects that any mind, human or synthetic, can read, write, and verify.

Three arguments braided through the whole book:

• Causalontology is important TO AGI: the faculties AGI needs - planning, imagination, transfer, explanation, agency - are all operations OVER causal structure, and today's systems have nowhere to keep it.

• Causalontology is an ENABLER of AGI: it does not compete with neural networks or language models; it gives them the missing organ - a shared, verifiable world model layer - and it climbs Pearl's ladder by data structure rather than by hope.

• Causalontology is important IN GENERAL: signed, provenance-first causal knowledge is what science, medicine, engineering, and a misinformation-soaked civilization need, with or without AGI.

PART ONE - THE WALL (why the current road does not reach AGI)

Chapter 1. The Age of Miracles That Cannot Reason.

• The paradox of 2026: systems that write sonnets and pass bar exams, yet fail at cause and effect a child masters at a light switch.

• Grounding from the current literature: state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs) score an average F1 of roughly 0.535 at inferring causal relationships from real-world text; performance collapses as causal information becomes implicit.

• Next-token prediction captures statistical patterns, not mechanisms - the correlation engine at civilization's center.

Chapter 2. The Ladder They Cannot Climb.

• Judea Pearl's Ladder of Causation: association, intervention, counterfactual.

• The verdict from the causal-inference community: machine learning remains at rung one; LLMs are sophisticated statistical approximators stuck at the bottom.

• Why scale does not climb ladders: more association is still association.

Chapter 3. The Billion-Dollar Admission.

• The field's own pivot: world-model startups with billion-dollar seeds; deep-learning pioneers conceding that symbol manipulation is necessary; the neurosymbolic turn now mainstream.

• The concession read honestly: everyone now agrees SOMETHING is missing - the fight is over what.

• Our answer: what is missing is not another model; it is a missing DATA STRUCTURE.

Chapter 4. The Black Box Crisis.

• You cannot align what you cannot read: interpretability as the bottleneck of safety.

• Explanations bolted on after the fact versus explanations that ARE the representation.

• The stakes: minds we can audit versus minds we can only hope about.

PART TWO - THE MISSING LINK (what Causalontology is)

Chapter 5. What If Causation Were a Thing?

• Reification, explained for everyone: turning "A causes B" from a sentence into an OBJECT with fields - causes, effects, mechanism, timing, modality, context, lineage.

• The Causal Relation Object: the atom of understanding - and, in 2.0.0, the object that now carries skips, so a cause can be traced across non-adjacent levels of description.

Chapter 6. A Verb-First, Noun-Hosting Ontology.

• Reality is what happens; things are its participants.

• Governing the verbs, hosting the nouns; the realizable hinge (fragility is only fully itself in the shattering) - and, in 2.0.0, the realizable now carries an identity-bearing label, a defect repaired.

• Why verb-first inverts the entire noun-first history of computing - databases, objects, knowledge graphs.

Chapter 7. Seventeen Kinds of Thing.

• Where 1.0.0 had eight kinds, 2.0.0 has seventeen, arranged in three tiers: nine type-tier content kinds, four token-tier content kinds, and four provenance kinds.

• Nine type-tier content kinds - pure, immutable, content-addressed - the laws and the furniture of the world:

  • occurrent - a happening; may now be pitched at a particular stratum (level of description).

  • Causal Relation Object - the reified causing; now carries skips (layer-skipping causation).

  • continuant - a thing that persists through change.

  • realizable - a disposition; now carries an identity-bearing label (a defect repair).

  • stratum - a level of description; the new answer to "at what level does this claim sit?"

  • bridge - a cross-stratal identity map: the assertion that one coarse occurrent IS a set of finer ones; the same happening seen at two levels.

  • port - a typed interface on a continuant.

  • conduit - a typed connection, port-to-port; a pipe or a computer, decided by the Perfect Wire Test.

  • quality - a property type.

• Four token-tier content kinds - LOCAL BY DEFAULT - the history of what actually happened, not merely what the laws permit:

  • token_individual - a particular thing.

  • token_occurrence - a particular happening at absolute time.

  • state_assertion - a value holding over an interval.

  • token_causal_claim - a particular causing under a covering law, carrying a token counterfactual.

• Four provenance kinds - signed, add-only:

  • assertion - now cites token evidence via evidenced_by; evidence ladder intervention > observation > simulation.

  • enrichment - now also offers occurrent_subsumes / occurrent_part_of.

  • retraction - the honest exit.

  • succession - institutional memory.

• The load-bearing separation persists at every tier: content versus provenance, uniformly; disagreement lives in provenance, never in contested cells.

Chapter 8. The Four Things 2.0.0 Added.

• 2.0.0 answers four questions 1.0.0 could not:

  • What LEVEL does a claim sit at? - the stratum.

  • Are two claims at different levels the SAME HAPPENING? - the bridge.

  • Are two things CONNECTED? - ports and conduits.

  • Did something ACTUALLY OCCUR - a history, not just laws? - the token tier.

• Levels and bridges: multi-scale causation made first-class; a neuron firing and a decision being made can be the same event bridged across strata; the amended Rule 7 makes reachability BRIDGED, so a chain may cross levels without breaking.

• Ports and conduits: connection made explicit; the Perfect Wire Test that decides pipe (carries without transforming) versus computer (transforms what it carries); typed interfaces that let mechanisms plug together.

• Layer-skipping causation and the skips flag: a cause may cross non-adjacent strata with NO intervening mechanism - and the ABSENCE of a mechanism is recorded as a positive finding, not a gap left blank.

• The new enabling modality: alongside cause and prevent, a relation may ENABLE - the making-possible that mechanism composition demands.

• Principle P7: whole words, never abbreviations - names spelled out in full across the standard.

Chapter 9. The Token Tier: A History, Not Only Laws.

• Type tier tells you what CAN happen; the token tier records what DID happen - a mind needs both.

• token_occurrence, state_assertion, token_causal_claim, token_individual: episodic memory as signed objects; the token counterfactual that lets a mind ask "what if THIS particular thing had not happened?"

• The clinical case: a single patient's history as token occurrences and state assertions over intervals, each token causal claim tied to a covering law yet particular to this life.

• Local by default: the token tier is private unless deliberately shared - privacy built into the data structure, not bolted on.

Chapter 10. Identity Without a Committee.

• Content addressing: the same claim minted anywhere on Earth gets the same identifier.

• Merge is set union; the commons is a Conflict-free Replicated Data Type (CRDT) by construction; no coordinator, no gatekeeper, no vote.

Chapter 11. Every Word Has an Author.

• Ed25519 signatures on every mutable datum, down to a single alias in a single language.

• Retraction as the honest exit; succession as institutional memory; framing made impossible.

• Acting beats watching: the evidence ladder with intervention on top - your own hand on the switch outranks any amount of staring - and, in 2.0.0, assertions cite the very token evidence they rest on via evidenced_by.

Chapter 13. The Faculties of a General Mind.

• Each faculty, shown as an OPERATION over the same seventeen kinds - one substrate, many faculties - now including reasoning up and down the strata through bridges and along conduits.

Chapter 14. The Glass Box Mind.

Chapter 15. The Commons: A World Model for Everyone.

Chapter 16. Commons Storage and Federation Design.

• The commons is a network, not a server - no single machine holds it, and by design none must; it is built on the Tier A / Tier B / Tier C store.

• Why the data wants to be a network: content-addressed identity plus merge-by-set-union is the same math as IPFS, Git, and Dat - a peer-to-peer federation converging with no coordinator.

• Tiers of participation: full nodes (hold everything), partial nodes (hold a slice; sharding is trivial because identity is a hash), light clients (hold almost nothing, verify locally, trust no one because every record is signed and self-certifying).

• What bounds the size: the commons federates LAWS (type tier), not diaries (token tier, local by default) - nearer Wikidata-scale than "every experience of every mind."

• The storage engine: immutable content-addressed objects give free deduplication, free integrity (the hash is the checksum), and permanent caching; a node is a blob store (SQLite / RocksDB / LMDB, or S3-compatible MinIO / Garage) plus a locally rebuilt index; hot/cold tiering guided by the stigmergy demand signal.

• Distribution and durability: signed snapshot dumps (like Wikidata dumps), shipped over IPFS, BitTorrent, or HTTPS, every byte verifiable by hash - global redundancy before a single live peer is recruited; a natural fit for the Internet Archive and Software Heritage.

• Reads and writes at scale: reads infinitely cacheable behind mirrors and a CDN; writes are mint-and-gossip with union merge and anti-entropy sync, no global lock.

• Trust and safety: signatures make trust a consumer policy, not forced consensus; token tier local-by-default preserves privacy; retraction without erasure; tamper-evidence from hashes and signatures alone.

• The phased build: (1) persistent content-addressed node - BUILT: the reference Tier A store is now durable (SQLite in WAL mode), idempotent by content address, and integrity-checked on write, with every endpoint and byte unchanged; (2) a genesis full node plus signed snapshot dumps - BUILT: a persistent node exports a deterministic, content-addressed snapshot committed by a Merkle root and signed with its Ed25519 key, with a detached checksum and signature, so anyone can verify every byte and stand up a mirror by union-merge - token tier excluded by default; (3) Tier B federation - gossip and anti-entropy; (4) light clients, CDN caching, and hash-prefix sharding for sideways scale. Phases one and two are delivered; phases three and four remain ahead.

• Free and open by design: federation spreads the cost with no central bill; institutional full nodes anchor durability; open license, open stack - cheap to mirror and impossible to quietly corrupt, by construction rather than charity.

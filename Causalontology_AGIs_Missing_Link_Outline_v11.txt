PART TWO - THE MISSING LINK (what Causalontology is)

Chapter 5. What If Causation Were a Thing?

• Reification, explained for everyone: turning "A causes B" from a sentence into an OBJECT with fields.

• The Causal Relation Object - the atom of understanding - and its fields, all present design:

◦ causes and effects - references to defined occurrent (process) types, never free text.

◦ mechanism - the finer causal objects that explain HOW, each a Causal Relation Object in its own right.

◦ temporal - the window, with fixed units down to a month defined as exactly 2,629,746 seconds; and the ordinal TICK as a separate, dimensionless dimension for processes lived in steps rather than seconds, never converted to the wall clock.

◦ modality - the flavor of the claim: necessary, sufficient, contributory, enabling (the cause makes the effect possible without bringing it about), or preventive.

◦ context, stratum (the level of description the claim is pitched at), and refines (lineage - so knowledge matures, not churns).

◦ skips - a flag declaring the cause reaches across NON-ADJACENT levels with no intervening mechanism claimed.

• Almost everything is optional: a degenerate claim ("pressing causes lighting") is perfectly legal - a partial claim is a standing invitation.

• The four things the reified claim cannot say on its own, answered by the object model:

◦ at what LEVEL a claim sits - the stratum.

◦ that two claims at different levels are the SAME HAPPENING - the bridge (identity, not correlation).

◦ that two things are CONNECTED - ports and conduits, decided pipe-versus-computer by the Perfect Wire Test.

◦ that something ACTUALLY OCCURRED - the token tier.

• The skip made honest: absence of a mechanism recorded as a positive FINDING (Principle P5), and the managed cross_stratal_seam its first-class form - mechanism_status "unmodeled" (exists, not drawn here) versus "absent" (there is none), an optional drawn chain, a home rule placing it at the coarsest level it touches. Rule 7 makes reachability BRIDGED, so a causal chain is checked for consistency across scales without pretending every skip is a staircase.

Chapter 6. A Verb-First, Noun-Hosting Ontology.

• Reality is what happens; things are its participants - the inversion of the entire noun-first history of computing (databases, objects, knowledge graphs).

• The atomic building block is the occurrent - the happening, the verb; the fundamental unit reifies the master verb, causation itself.

• Governing the verbs, hosting the nouns: a thin continuant backbone that defers domain depth to the specialist ontologies.

• The realizable hinge - dispositions, functions, and roles that inhere in things and discharge in happenings; fragility is only fully itself in the shattering. Each realizable carries an identity-bearing label, so one bearer may hold many distinguishable dispositions - fragile, catalytic, and soluble at once - without collapsing into one blur.

• Knowing is verb-first: knowledge enters by ACTING and by active watching, which the evidence ladder makes enforceable rather than merely poetic.

Chapter 7. Twenty-One Kinds of Thing.

• The entire universe of Causalontology contains exactly twenty-one kinds - a vocabulary you can learn before lunch and build a civilization's knowledge on after it: ten type-tier content, seven token-tier content, and four provenance.

• Ten TYPE-TIER content kinds - pure, immutable, content-addressed - the laws and furniture of the world:

◦ occurrent - a process or event TYPE (press_button, light_on); may be pitched at a named stratum.

◦ causal_relation_object - the reified causing, the atom of understanding; may carry a skips flag.

◦ continuant - a thing that endures (button, lamp, patient) - the hosted nouns.

◦ realizable - a disposition, function, or role, borne by a continuant, realized in occurrents; carries an identity-bearing label.

◦ stratum - a single level of description within a named scheme (molecular, cellular, social).

◦ bridge - a cross-stratal identity map: one coarse occurrent IS a set of finer ones, the same happening seen at two levels.

◦ cross_stratal_seam - a managed jump across NON-ADJACENT levels, recording in mechanism_status whether the intervening mechanism is unmodeled or absent, with an optional drawn chain and a home rule; the honest, first-class form of the skips flag.

◦ port - a typed interface borne by a continuant.

◦ conduit - a typed connection, port to port; a PIPE if it conveys unchanged, a COMPUTER if it transforms; may carry a realized_by reference by identity to the native law or signal that actually realizes the transform.

◦ quality - a property TYPE (mass, colour, temperature, charge).

• Seven TOKEN-TIER content kinds - LOCAL BY DEFAULT - not the law but the record of what particular things did, what a mind expects, and what one mind believes of another:

◦ token_individual - a single particular thing: THIS patient, this button.

◦ token_occurrence - a particular happening at an absolute time.

◦ state_assertion - a value holding over an interval.

◦ token_causal_claim - a particular causing under a covering law, carrying its own but-for counterfactual.

◦ attitude - what a modeled mind CONTAINS: a holder (a continuant or token_individual, never a signing key), a closed attitude_type (believes, desires, intends, knows, expects, fears), and content (a reference to ANY content object, including another attitude, which is how attributions nest). The content may be FALSE; it carries no strength of its own.

◦ predicted_occurrence - a happening a stated predictor EXPECTS: the occurrent type predicted, a window in exactly one dimension of time (wall clock or tick, never both, never neither), and an optional identity-bearing strength (the predicted probability). A prediction is not a report.

◦ prediction_error - pairs a predicted_occurrence with the token_occurrence that did or did not fulfil it, carrying the signed discrepancy (actual minus expected: positive exceeded, negative fell short, zero fulfilled); observed may be absent - the surprise of nothing arriving.

• Four PROVENANCE kinds - signed, add-only:

◦ assertion - who claims a content object is true, on what evidence, with what strength and confidence, when; may cite token evidence via evidenced_by.

◦ enrichment - who added one alias, participant, or taxonomy link; also occurrent_subsumes and occurrent_part_of.

◦ retraction - a source's honest withdrawal of its OWN earlier record.

◦ succession - a cryptographic change of guard: this new key continues that old key's identity and responsibilities.

• The load-bearing separation, uniform at every tier: content versus provenance. One immutable claim; a thousand signatures around it, growing it stronger; disagreement lives in provenance, never in contested cells - there is nothing to fight over.

• Three seams join symbolic structure to native dynamics, all current design: the tick lets lived time in; the cross_stratal_seam makes the leap between levels an honest managed record; and realized_by clasps a conduit to the living law that realizes it - structure and dynamics joined by one fingerprint, neither pretending to be the other.

Chapter 8. The Token Tier: A History, an Expected World, and Other Minds.

• Type tier tells you what CAN happen; the token tier records what DID happen, what a mind EXPECTS, and what one mind BELIEVES of another - a general mind needs all three.

• History: token_occurrence, state_assertion, token_causal_claim, token_individual - episodic memory as signed objects, with the token counterfactual that asks "what if THIS particular thing had not happened?"

• The clinical case: a single patient's history as token occurrences and state assertions over intervals, each token causal claim tied to a covering law yet particular to this life.

• Native time: a timing-critical process kept in TICKS - a discrete, dimensionless ordinal step, never distorted into wall-clock seconds it never owned; ticks and seconds are disjoint dimensions and never convert.

• Other minds, made data: the attitude records what a holder's mind contains, not what is true. Rule 25, the doxastic quarantine - content that contradicts the actual record raises NO conflict, because that mismatch IS a false belief, first-class and shareable; the holder stays forever distinct from the signing source. Theory of mind becomes a mind that can be WRONG about another mind, on the record.

• The expected world: the predicted_occurrence gives forecasts a home, and the prediction_error gives the comparator's signed surprise a home. Rule 24 - a prediction is NOT a report: a forecast gets a different identifier from a token_occurrence of comparable content, and a paired observation must instantiate the same occurrent.

• Local by default: the token tier is private unless deliberately published - privacy built into the data structure, not bolted on; the attitude doubly so, an attribution of a mental state among the most sensitive records the standard can carry.

Chapter 9. Identity Without a Committee.

• Content addressing: an object's name is the Secure Hash Algorithm 256-bit (SHA-256) fingerprint of its identity-bearing fields in canonical form (RFC 8785) - the same claim minted anywhere on Earth gets the same identifier.

• The digest is taken over fields PRESENT and nothing else, so every optional field is identity-preserving: a record keeps its exact name across the life of the standard.

• Merge is set union; the commons is a Conflict-free Replicated Data Type (CRDT) by construction; no coordinator, no gatekeeper, no vote. Tamper-evidence is free: alter one byte and identity no longer matches content.

Chapter 10. Every Word Has an Author.

• Ed25519 signatures on every mutable datum, down to a single alias in a single language - every author checkable arithmetic; no anonymous edit, no framing.

• Retraction as the honest exit; succession as institutional memory.

• Acting beats watching: the evidence ladder with intervention on top - intervention over observation over simulation, then derivation, human hint, and imported - and assertions cite the very token evidence they rest on via evidenced_by.

Chapter 15. Commons Storage and Federation Design.

• The commons is a network, not a server - no single machine holds it, and by design none must; built on the Tier A / Tier B / Tier C store.

• Why the data wants to be a network: content-addressed identity plus merge-by-set-union is the same math as the InterPlanetary File System (IPFS), Git, and Dat - a peer-to-peer federation converging with no coordinator.

• Tiers of participation: full nodes (hold everything), partial nodes (hold a hash-prefix slice; sharding is trivial because identity is a hash), light clients (hold almost nothing, verify locally, trust no one because every record is signed and self-certifying).

• What bounds the size: the commons federates LAWS (type tier), not diaries (token tier, local by default) - nearer Wikidata-scale than "every experience of every mind."

• The storage engine: immutable content-addressed objects give free deduplication, free integrity (the hash is the checksum), and permanent caching; a node is a blob store (SQLite / RocksDB / LMDB, or S3-compatible MinIO / Garage) plus a locally rebuilt index; hot/cold tiering guided by the stigmergy demand signal.

• Distribution and durability: signed snapshot dumps, shipped over IPFS, BitTorrent, or Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure (HTTPS), every byte verifiable by hash - global redundancy before a single live peer is recruited; a natural fit for the Internet Archive and Software Heritage.

• Reads and writes at scale: reads infinitely cacheable behind mirrors and a content-delivery network (CDN); writes are mint-and-gossip with union merge and anti-entropy sync, no global lock.

• Trust and safety: signatures make trust a consumer policy, not forced consensus; token tier local-by-default preserves privacy; retraction without erasure; tamper-evidence from hashes and signatures alone.

• The phased build, delivered: (1) a persistent content-addressed node - durable (SQLite in write-ahead logging (WAL) mode), idempotent by content address, integrity-checked on write; (2) a genesis full node plus signed snapshot dumps - a deterministic snapshot committed by a Merkle root and signed with an Ed25519 key, verifiable byte for byte, token tier excluded by default; (3) Tier B federation - gossip and background anti-entropy that compares Merkle roots and ships only the delta, every inbound item verified by hash and signature before merge, add-only, no coordinator; (4) light clients, immutable-cache CDN headers with an entity tag (ETag) equal to the identifier, and hash-prefix sharding for sideways scale. ALL FOUR PHASES ARE DELIVERED.

• What remains is not architecture but adoption: standing up genesis and partner nodes and growing the commons - free and open by construction, cheap to mirror and impossible to quietly corrupt.

APPENDIX 1 - THE SPECIFICATION, COMPLETE AND TERSE.

• Specification 4.0.0, frozen, git tag v4.0.0 - a programming-language-neutral standard, a data structure and its rules, not a program.

• The twenty-one kinds: ten type-tier content (occurrent, causal_relation_object, continuant, realizable, stratum, bridge, cross_stratal_seam, port, conduit, quality), seven token-tier content and LOCAL BY DEFAULT (token_individual, token_occurrence, state_assertion, token_causal_claim, attitude, predicted_occurrence, prediction_error), and four provenance and signed (assertion, enrichment, retraction, succession).

• The identity procedure: an identifier is scheme:localpart, the localpart the lowercase hexadecimal SHA-256 digest of the identity-bearing fields present, canonicalized under RFC 8785 - two authors anywhere who state the same content mint the same identifier and merge with no coordinator.

• The seven principles P1 through P7: no free-floating tokens; tokens local by default; levels scheme-relative; content bears no provenance (the one recorded exception being a predicted_occurrence's strength, predicted content, not a provenance evaluation); absence made positive; every addition forced by a recorded failure; whole words, never abbreviations.

• The five modalities: necessary, sufficient, contributory, enabling, preventive.

• Time: eight wall-clock units with fixed conversion constants (a month is exactly 2,629,746 seconds, a year 31,556,952), plus the ordinal TICK - a dimensionless step, held as a dimension disjoint from the wall clock, never converted.

• The twenty-five semantic rules, including Rule 7 (bridged reachability), Rule 22 (cross-stratal seam well-formedness), Rule 23 (ordinal units are a separate dimension), Rule 24 (a prediction is not a report), and Rule 25 (the doxastic quarantine); plus the one store rule for deterministic cycle-breaking under replication.

• The six normative algorithms A through F; the evidence grades (intervention over observation over simulation, then derivation, human hint, imported); the gap taxonomy (hard failures versus stigmergic invitations); the complete abstract store operation set and the Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) reference binding; thirty locked decisions; and conformance against all 137 vectors, V01 through V137.

• The token-tier safety rules R1 through R6 - laws, not diaries - and the honest limits: an unforgetting store, key recovery a governance act, takedown stated in advance by tier.

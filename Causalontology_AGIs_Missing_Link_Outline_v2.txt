CAUSALONTOLOGY: AGI'S MISSING LINK

THE MANIFESTO - OUTLINE

For THE AGI 2026 conference, San Francisco.

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator and Owner of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Working thesis, held as the frame of every chapter: Causalontology is important to achieve Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) - not as one more model, but as the missing DATA STRUCTURE: causation itself, reified into signed, mergeable, inspectable objects that any mind, human or synthetic, can read, write, and verify.

Three arguments braided through the whole book:

• Causalontology is important TO AGI: the faculties AGI needs - planning, imagination, transfer, explanation, agency - are all operations OVER causal structure, and today's systems have nowhere to keep it.

• Causalontology is an ENABLER of AGI: it does not compete with neural networks or language models; it gives them the missing organ - a shared, verifiable world model layer - and it climbs Pearl's ladder by data structure rather than by hope.

• Causalontology is important IN GENERAL: signed, provenance-first causal knowledge is what science, medicine, engineering, and a misinformation-soaked civilization need, with or without AGI.

PART ONE - THE WALL (why the current road does not reach AGI)

Chapter 1. The Age of Miracles That Cannot Reason.

• The paradox of 2026: systems that write sonnets and pass bar exams, yet fail at cause and effect a child masters at a light switch.

• Grounding from the current literature: state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs) score an average F1 of roughly 0.535 at inferring causal relationships from real-world text; performance collapses as causal information becomes implicit.

• Next-token prediction captures statistical patterns, not mechanisms - the correlation engine at civilization's center.

Chapter 2. The Ladder They Cannot Climb.

• Judea Pearl's Ladder of Causation: association, intervention, counterfactual.

• The verdict from the causal-inference community: machine learning remains at rung one; LLMs are sophisticated statistical approximators stuck at the bottom.

• Why scale does not climb ladders: more association is still association.

Chapter 3. The Billion-Dollar Admission.

• The field's own pivot: world-model startups with billion-dollar seeds; deep-learning pioneers conceding that symbol manipulation is necessary; the neurosymbolic turn now mainstream.

• The concession read honestly: everyone now agrees SOMETHING is missing - the fight is over what.

• Our answer: what is missing is not another model; it is a missing DATA STRUCTURE.

Chapter 4. The Black Box Crisis.

• You cannot align what you cannot read: interpretability as the bottleneck of safety.

• Explanations bolted on after the fact versus explanations that ARE the representation.

• The stakes: minds we can audit versus minds we can only hope about.

PART TWO - THE MISSING LINK (what Causalontology is)

Chapter 5. What If Causation Were a Thing?

• Reification, explained for everyone: turning "A causes B" from a sentence into an OBJECT with fields - causes, effects, mechanism, timing, modality, context, lineage.

• The Causal Relation Object (CRO): the atom of understanding.

Chapter 6. A Verb-First, Noun-Hosting Ontology.

• Reality is what happens; things are its participants.

• Governing the verbs, hosting the nouns; the realizable hinge (fragility is only fully itself in the shattering).

• Why verb-first inverts the entire noun-first history of computing - databases, objects, knowledge graphs.

Chapter 7. Eight Kinds of Thing.

• Four content kinds (occurrent, CRO, continuant, realizable) - pure, immutable, content-addressed.

• Four provenance kinds (assertion, enrichment, retraction, succession) - signed, add-only.

• The load-bearing separation: content versus provenance, uniformly; disagreement lives in provenance, never in contested cells.

Chapter 8. Identity Without a Committee.

• Content addressing: the same claim minted anywhere on Earth gets the same identifier.

• Merge is set union; the commons is a Conflict-free Replicated Data Type (CRDT) by construction; no coordinator, no gatekeeper, no vote.

Chapter 9. Every Word Has an Author.

• Ed25519 signatures on every mutable datum, down to a single alias in a single language.

• Retraction as the honest exit; succession as institutional memory; framing made impossible.

• Acting beats watching: the evidence ladder with intervention on top - your own hand on the switch outranks any amount of staring.

PART THREE - THE ENABLER (how Causalontology powers AGI)

Chapter 10. Climbing the Ladder by Data Structure.

• Rung one lives in the assertion's evidence grades; rung two lives in intervention-graded CROs; rung three lives in refinement, context, and quarantined imagined realities.

• The ladder is not climbed by bigger models but by a representation in which every rung is a first-class object.

Chapter 11. The Faculties of a General Mind.

• Planning is search over causal chains; imagination is counterfactual composition of CROs; transfer is analogy over relation structure; explanation is reading the mechanism field aloud; agency is choosing interventions; memory consolidation is refinement lineage.

• Each faculty, shown as an OPERATION over the same eight kinds - one substrate, many faculties.

Chapter 12. The Glass Box Mind.

• Alignment transformed: a mind whose beliefs are signed, inspectable causal objects can be audited claim by claim.

• "Why?" always has a readable answer - by construction, not by interpretability archaeology.

Chapter 13. The Commons: A World Model for Everyone.

• One shared, provenance-first store of causal knowledge - Wikidata's lesson, specialized to causation.

• Federation and offline replication; convergence without consensus machinery.

Chapter 14. Stigmergy: The Wall That Teaches.

• The half-built wall shows the next worker where the next brick goes.

• Six gap kinds, value-ranked; the store announces its own most valuable next contribution; demand measured from real readership.

Chapter 15. The First Gardener.

• The receipts from the reference implementation: perfect public-training-set scores on the first two Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus benchmarks (ARC-AGI-1: 400 of 400; ARC-AGI-2: 120 of 120) by pure symbolic induction, no LLM - stated precisely, never inflated.

• The live demonstration: a glass-box synthetic mind (Mentova, on PrologAI) reads the gap list, intervenes a hundred times with its own hand on the switch, induces the missing fields, signs an intervention-grade assertion, and the gap closes on everyone's frontier.

• The first brick laid in the shared wall by a machine - and every step of it narrated.
